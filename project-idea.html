Project Idea

Internet Profile Management for Deceased Persons

Overview

Beau’s project idea is a new tailored solution for identity management after you or your loved one has deceased. IMaaS (Identity Management as a Service) would usher about a new era to ensure that loved ones can choose to either delete your entire internet profile or retain and manage under their request. Additionally, this service would reduce the emotional toll searching for multiple social media accounts linked to the deceased.

Motivation

At a minimum, 300 million new social media accounts are created every year. One of the most difficult times for loved ones, is managing the deceased affairs, ranging from estates to their online digital footprint. Whilst taking into consideration that the average person has 7 social media accounts, it can be quite degrading and upsetting for friends and family of the recently deceased trying to either delete or manage their social media accounts. With the project idea I propose, the idea would have one account linked to all social media, in which a deceased persons affair can be managed centrally rather than individual accounts.

Description

The project functionality is to create a profile on our website portal, which users can associate all their social media logins (either from OAuth or having their credentials stored in plain text). This data would be only available to those who have an authenticated login for that session data.

Security being one of our biggest concerns would be enforced using MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication). This will require users to have either a mobile app like Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, MFA to Text Service (Which users will need to register their Mobile Phone) or authentication via email.

One of the core services and functionality surrounding the website portal would be the ease of transaction for family of a recently deceased and transparency. To maximize the experience this solution provides, we’d request that all users when signing up have their website portal listed in their will, this way the nominated beneficiary can contact us to assist with providing access to their deceased persons profile, update their profile and have their deceased certificate deployed to all social media accounts from one location via the provided the legal documentation.

This service proactively resolves issues that become apparent such as, requiring a court order from the United States (for some social media), having to send multiple emails to different social media platforms and managing these cases.

Throughout the project, I have identified some potential drawbacks to this service. Some examples can be: Do I charge for such a service? If so, how much? What services exist like this already? An example of this is that Google email accounts have an option called “Inactive Account Manager” which can delete the account within a nominated time of inactivity. Additionally, as stated previously, all social media platforms do have an option to request for a deceased person account to be deleted or have access to on behalf of the lawful representative of the deceased person

Tools and Technologies

  • Web Hosting
  • 0Auth Technologies
  • MFA Security

Skills Required

  • HTML / CSS
  • Programing and Software Engineering
  • International Legal Counsel
  • Interpersonal Softskills
  • Business Management

Outcome

If this project is successful, families would be able to manage the deceased social media accounts more seamlessly without having to fight for access to these accounts with the social media provider. I do find it unlikely that such a service like this would ever likely be successfully implemented due to its controversial and difficult nature, however, I do believe that the intent behind the service is justified and were to be used in good will.
